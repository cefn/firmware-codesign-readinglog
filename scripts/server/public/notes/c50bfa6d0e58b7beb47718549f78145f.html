Reducing Natural-Language Ambiguities in Requirements Engineering

Authors

Lars Schnieder and Susanne Arndt

Notes

They describe the deployment of an automated system to uncover ambiguities and vagueness within technical specification documents.

Concepts

Quotations

"Requirements engineering is a communication process that involves understanding and coordinating terminologies from different
disciplines."

"[Requirements] are discussed and modified over time to arrive as far as possible at a...specification that is testable, unambiguous, complete, and correct."

"Terminology that belongs to one knowledge system is often misunderstood by people who work with another knowledge system."

"Lexical ambiguity refers to single expressions that may be reasonably interpreted in more than one way."

"[Syntactic ambiguities occur] when pronouns and relative clauses can refer back to two or more other elements of the preceding sentence ...[leading] to different interpretations"

"Discourse ambiguities are defined as incompatibility between several requirements"

"vagueness [where it is] difficult to form any interpretation at the desired level of specificity. This is in contrast to ambiguity, where more than one interpretation is evoked."

"correcting faults requires close cooperation between linguists and domain experts whose knowledge is required to recognize and remedy linguistic faults"
