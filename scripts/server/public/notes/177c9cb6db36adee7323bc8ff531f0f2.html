Using joint application design to develop readable formal specifications

Authors

Robert B. Jackson, David W. Embley

Status

Scanned

Relevance

Accuses established design processes of using 'computerese' as a specification language and militates for something better. Directly linked to boundary objects and comprehensibility for non-programmers.

Quotations

"The goal of the JAD
approach is to produce higher quality software in an
accelerated time by having both users and analysts
accept responsibility and become heavily involved in
the system development. The JAD approach is con-
ducted through a series of meetings called JAD sessions."

"We identify three major types of activities in this process:
One activity is the analysis, which is a discovery and
model building activity. Another activity is prototype
execution, which is a verification and refinement activity.
The third activity is specification development, which
also includes verification and refinement, and additionally encompasses documentation and ‘contractual’ agreement. One of the major contributions of our
approach is the integration of JAD with the development
of formal specifications."
