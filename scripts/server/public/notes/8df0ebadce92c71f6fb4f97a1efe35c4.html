"The purpose of this special issue is therefore to revisit the concept [of Boundary Object] in order to look at its analytical scope whilst taking care to maintain the close articulation between interpretive flexibility and infrastructure."

"One of the first to return to the notion was Kathryn Henderson in an anthropology of the design activity (Henderson, 1991, 1998a). She is particularly interested in visual representations – drawings and diagrams – produced to organise discussions and move collective work forward. These are elements which facilitate the distributed cognitive process because they facilitate the crossover between interpretations held by the different professional groups involved in the design of a turbine. In her work (Henderson, 1998b), she explores the impact of computer-assisted design tools on visual design culture."
