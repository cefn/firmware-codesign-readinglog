Pattern Languages in Interaction Design: Structure and Organization

Authors

Martijn van Welie, Gerrit C. van der Veer

Status

Unread

Relevance

Provides a potential boundary object for firmware co-design - that of interaction design patterns perhaps evoked by an archetypal example of its use, which participants may already be familiar with, or could be exposed to. It also provides a model of the way in which design alternatives could be structured and navigated according to relationships such as is-a or has-a or relates-to.

Notes

They make explicit the assumption that interaction design for them involves a "2D display", when discussing how whole solutions might break down into individual patterns. In the architectural case this can be done according to spatial scale. They have ambitions for a hierarchy which isn't based on the designs occupying a metric space. The transference of patterns from architecture to 'design patterns' in object-oriented software design is instructive. It's interesting, however, that something intended to help with engagement (clients being able to refer to patterns) has ended up within OO being about arcane implementation detail where the design alternatives and benefits are principally understood by programmers and relate to implementation practices, rather than end-results.
