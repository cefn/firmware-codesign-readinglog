The Brave New World of Design Requirements

Authors

Mathias Jarke, Pericles Loucopoulos, Kalle Lyytinen, John Mylopoulos, William Robinson

Notes

 Their tripartite analysis of the requirements process is useful perhaps, to differentiate scope or stages in workshop processes.

Their observation that RE researchers focus on the what rather than the how, may reveal one of the core issues here. Whilst specifying 'what' can appear to be a complete description, once you embark on the 'how' (having to implement the 'what'), other unspecified aspects emerge, which in practice project back onto the 'what' - e.g. interactions required in the device which are too complex to be feasible in the field, incorrect defaults which take no account of users' needs. This may link to the notion of 'non-functional' requirements (see wikipedia).

"we adopt a widely employed and straightforward categorization of the requirements processes into three facets: (1) discovery, (2) specification,
and (3) validation and verification  During discovery, designers develop an understanding of the application domain and infer specific design needs through consultation with stakeholders and other sources [18]. Requirements specification is both a noun and a verb in RE parlance. As a noun, a specification is the document in which the requirements for a design effort are articulated thus representing an agreement between the stakeholders
and the design team [16,24]. The verb suggests the process of developing and managing the specification document; it is the process by which the design team acquires, abstracts, and represents the requirements [17,22]. Finally, during requirements validation and verification designers ensure that the requirements are (1) of high quality, (2) by addressing the users’ needs, (3) and being appropriate for the design effort, and (4) having no
inconsistencies or defects"

"Requirements researchers have also stated that requirements are properly concerned with what is to be achieved by a design artifact,
without regard to how it will be designed and implemented"
