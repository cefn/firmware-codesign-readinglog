The Design of Sites

Authors

Douglas K. van Duyne, Dr. James A. Landay, and Jason I. Hong

Status

Scanned

Relevance

Highly relevant as it connects interaction design and pattern language as a mechanism for referencing and considering alternative design choices appropriate for the early, divergent, design phase, using techniques intended for collaborative design. Reports on a worked example of a pattern language for websites as a domain of interaction design.

Quotations

"Patterns, he said, can empower people by providing a
living and shared language...Alexander intended for patterns to be used by everyday people to guide the process
of creation, whether designing a house for themselves or working with others designing offices
and public spaces. By creating a common language, would-be designers could discuss and take
part in the design of the spaces in which they worked, lived, and played. Alexander’s patterns
were also a reaction against contemporary architectural design, which he felt did not take enough
of human needs, nature, growth, spirituality, and community into consideration." £ strong resonance with our ambitions for firmware co-design

"As in Alexander’s pattern language, each pattern is connected to certain higher-level
patterns and to certain lower-level patterns. The pattern helps complete the higher-level patterns
that are “above” it, and it is completed itself by the lower-level patterns that are “below” it."

Notes

A summary chapter extract from a book which has a more comprehensive worked pattern language for websites.
