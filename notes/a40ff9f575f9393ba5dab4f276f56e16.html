Concrete Abstractions

Authors

Max Hailperin, Barbara Kaiser, Karl Knight

Notes

Referred to by Bratteteig. Offers a set of abstractions for computation, which are presented as educational, but also perhaps exhaustive of the space of possible computations.

 

Field of Study

Concepts

Quotations

"This book is divided into three parts, dealing with procedural abstractions, data
abstractions, and abstractions of state.

"A procedure is a way of abstracting what’s called a computational process. Roughly speaking, a process is a dynamic succession of events—a happening."

"Data is the information processed by computational processes, not only the externally visible information, but also the internal information structures used within the processes."

"State is anything that can be changed by one part of a computation in order to have an effect on a later part of the computation."
