Procuring Usable Systems - An Analysis of a Commercial Procurement Project

Authors

Erik Markensten

Notes

They follow through a procurement process to try and figure out where the usability part happens and who has responsibility for it (supplier or procurer). They find that it falls between the gaps, and propose engaging much earlier with the 'how' and usability questions, and engaging professionals to do so, because the procurement staff, untrained in usability, found confronting usability issues hard .

"the user-centred design process can be seen as divided into two stages. The first stage, the “what” stage, aims at defining what kind of system and functionality that will solve identified user needs. The second stage, the “how” stage, addresses how the interaction with the identified functionality should be designed."
