Contents and Structure in Design Reasoning

Authors

Gabriela Goldschmidt and Maya Weil

Notes

Tags

Relevance

Models the cognitive processes involved at the divergent phase of a design process, which the authors characterise as 'sketchy' and linked to exploring multiple possibilities. They adopt the perspective of design as 'moves' in a space or game and have a corresponding analysis of transcript data. There is detailed discussion of methodology choice and self-critique of their think-aloud protocol.

Quotations

"The present study
focuses on design reasoning at the early, "front edge" phase of
designing in which a solution to a design problem is sought at a
preliminary, sketchy level. In this phase, designers are engaged in a
search in which many ideas and possibilities are typically raised
and examined, often to be discarded later."

"The present study investigates the
relationship-assuming there is one-between contents and structure
in design reasoning. By contents, we mean the context-bound subject
matters that are being considered, such as rooms and walls in the
case of architectural design, or metal tubes and plastic casings in the
case of industrial design. By structure, we mean consistent internal
relationships and patterns that can be detected in the cognitive
operations involved in design thinking."

"We propose an investigation
of online records of design behavior, parsed into individual design
moves."

"In design as in other fields, most protocol analysis studies
are based on "think aloud" experiments in which individuals
engage in the investigated activity while they continuously verbalize their thoughts."

"Linkography calls for the division of a protocol into units (by
subject matter), which are then parsed into design moves. Typically,
a unit comprises between twenty and one hundred moves. A move
can be comprehended as follows: "The meaning of "move" in
designing is akin to its meaning in chess: a design move is a step, an
act, an operation, which transforms the design situation relative to
the state in which it was prior to that move."

"We focus our attention on the links that moves form among
themselves, because we postulate that they are the key to the under-
standing of design reasoning."

"the intricate process of design reason-
ing is nonlinear, in the sense that, at least as far as significant (criti-
cal) design moves are concerned, every step is double speared: it
moves forward but also makes sure that it is congruous with what
has already been achieved, and it validates what has been done thus
far with an eye on ways to proceed from that point"
