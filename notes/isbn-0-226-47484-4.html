Quotations

"His universe of instruments is closed, and the rules of his game are always to make do with ‘whatever is at hand’, that is to say with a set of tools and materials which is always finite and is also heterogeneous because it...is the contingent result of all the occasions there have been to renew or enrich the stock or to maintain it with the remains of previous constructions or destructions”

"the 'bricoleur' ...'speaks' not only with things as we have already seen, but also through the medium of things: giving an account of his personality and life by the choices he makes between the limited possibilities"

"We have already distinguished the scientist and the 'bricoleur' by the invese functions which they assign to events and structures as ends and means, the scientist creating events (changing the world) by means of structures and the 'bricoleur' creating structures by means of events"

"On a different plane we therefore find once more this dialogue with the materials and means of execution by which we defined 'bricolage'"
