Formulating Usability

Authors

Harold Thimbleby

Notes

 Various formal measures of usability for a system described as a state machine connected by a graph of transitions.

Field of Study

Concepts

Graph theory, State Machine, Usability, Formalism, Complexity

Quotations

"The motivation of the present work is to make a contribution to user interface design, specifically to discuss ways of measuring usability from design, from specifications rather than implementations."

"Ideally, of course, user interfaces should be empirically evaluated and then improved, but in practice many products are designed and then fobbed off to users, with little opportunity for improvements."

"This paper suggests treating user interfaces as finite state machines, and using graph theory as an analytic framework. The approach is not contentious, but means that many (even obvious) faults in user interfaces will not be addressed."

"although there may be a direct way to give the user a model, doing so can be assumed to interfere with the user's task"

"we may be safe in assuming that the larger the length of a complete manual is, the less usable the system"

"much complexity arises from pedantry in what we wish to describe. If users can be assumed to understand the numbers involved in times, then we do not need to describe every possible change of time: instead all times can be treated equivalently. The alarm clock becomes about 3 augmented states."

"We have argued that very many direct measures of usability can be obtained at design time. These measures have to be used judiciously, but they are easy to obtain and give direct indicators of usability."
