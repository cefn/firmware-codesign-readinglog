"Broadly, functional requirements define what a system is supposed todowhereas non-functional requirements define how a system is supposed tobe. Functional requirements are usually in the form of "system shall do <requirement>", while non-functional requirements are "system shall be <requirement>".

Non-functional requirements are often calledqualitiesof a system. Other terms for non-functional requirements are "constraints", "quality attributes", "quality goals", "quality of service requirements" and "non-behavioral requirements". [1] Informally these are sometimes called the "ilities", from attributes like stability and portability. Qualities, that is non-functional requirements, can be divided into two main categories:

  1. Execution qualities, such as security and usability, which are observable at run time.
  2. Evolution qualities, such astestability, maintainability, extensibility and scalability, which are embodied in the static structure of the software system. [2] [3] "
