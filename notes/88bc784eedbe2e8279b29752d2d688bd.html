"Brainstorming (Osborn, 1957) is an intervention in which individuals, groups, and organizations adhere to a set of four rules while working in sessions designated to generate ideas. The rules are (1) to generate as many ideas as possible, (2) to avoid criticizing any of the ideas, (3) to attempt to combine and improve on previously articulated ideas, and (4) to encourage the generation of “wild” ideas."
