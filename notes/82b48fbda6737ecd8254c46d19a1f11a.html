The Productive Role of Material Design Artefacts in Participatory Design Events

Notes

Explores the possible relevant qualities which artefacts can have within design events.

Concepts

inquiring materials [credited to Gednryd]

interactive cognition [credited to Gednryd]

Quotations

"we focus on how different design situations evolve as an interplay among participants, things, surroundings, and social constructs...it draws our attention to how participants in a design event frame and reframe their understanding, and resolve problematic situations through the use of design materials."

"Material design artefacts are employed in a wide range of collaborative design events, for instance mock-ups [8] probes [15], props [2] and card-based design games, such as Inspiration Card Workshops [4]."

References

Gedenryd (1998) "How designers work: Cognitive studies." - the concept of inquiring materials

Bertelsen, O. W. Elements of a Theory of Design Artefacts – a contribution to critical systems development research. [PhD] 'Bertelsen’s approach directs our attention to the fact that design artefacts often act as go-betweens for the various participants in design experiments.'

Lim et al. 2008 "The anatomy of prototypes: Prototypes as filters, prototypes as manifestations of design ideas."

Ehn and Kyng (1992) "Cardboard computers: mocking-it-up or hands-on the future." 'we find Kyng's approach valuable, as it highlights the role of material design artefacts by focusing on the interplay between material aspects and cooperation'
