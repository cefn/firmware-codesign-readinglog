The House of Quality

Authors

John R. Hauser, Don Clausing

Notes

A Harvard Business Review paper, characterising a matrix representation to help choosing from potentially competing feature sets within a product development process, to try and settle on a best match against customer requirements.

The approach is centred on the 'Voice of the customer' but it's not obvious that this translates into any kind of user engagement.

An interesting aspect of the House of Quality matrix representation (illustrated by Exhibit XI in the paper) is that it can potentially decompose recursively to get more and more detailed (where the hows become whats). It suggests that in a suitable problem domain it could handle very small aspects, right the way up to the strategic direction. This is illustrated

The statement which describes how it mediates between marketing, engineers, and general managers positions it as a clear boundary object.
