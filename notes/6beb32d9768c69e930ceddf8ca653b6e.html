Can sociologists understand other forms of life?

Authors

Rachel Cooper

Notes

 Written by Rachel Cooper (who studied with me at Nottingham). She proposes (through sceptical attacks on other candidate approaches) that cross the gap of understanding to an alien form of life (for example sociologists trying to understand natural scientists) that the anthropological embedding technique is the single way she can identify for a researcher to have a meaningful encounter with another form of life, and that an empathetic account of anthropological embedding is the only thing which can help someone who can't directly have the encounter (the reader is expected to empathise with the anthropological character in the anthro-novel, so they can arrive at that path-dependent knowledge through elements they have in common) .

Quotations

"I have argued that there is a method that might enable sociologists of scientific knowledge to gain understanding of an alien form of life and to pass on this understanding to their readers. The sociologists would first have to gain understanding of the form of life themselves through enculturation. ...If the sociologists of science succeed in becoming enculturated then their next challenge is to create an anthro-novel."

"The bad news is that so far as I know no Sociologist of Scientific Knowledge has written an anthro-novel to date. As such those existing case studies that claim to enable readers to understand scientists belonging to different forms of life cannot be doing this. The techniques that their authors use to try and enable the aliens to be understood are simply not up to the job. "
